After losing my sense of purpose during service in the Royal Air Force, I turned my attention back to my historic passion: Astronomy! Whilst undertaking my undergraduate research at Birkeck College, I fell in love with all things exoplanetary, and regained my direction in life. The idea that all of these exoplanets are out in the cosmos, orbiting their stars, and just waiting for us to come along and find them had me totally hooked! To further my goals, I am currently undertaking an MSc in Space Exploration Systems at the University of Leicester. My ultimate goal is to undertake a PhD in exoplanet detection or the development of instrumentation specifically tailored to identifying the smallest, most challenging exoplanets to observe. If you share similar interests or just want to connect — connect with me on LinkedIn!

Away from exoplanets, I am a keen trail runner with the long term goal of running the Western States 100, the Moab 240, and UTMB. Sport is a huge passion, and I am a loyal (and long suffering) supporter of Southampton FC, as well as closely following Forumla 1, and Cricket. My partner and I run a small travel channel on YouTube for fun!

My Research

Active Project

Heliophyics Spacecraft Mission

Interstellar Medium Mission Concept focussed on investigating the plasma processes and boundary dynamics of the heliosphere, and its interaction with the Local Interstellar Medium. This project is split between the University of Leicester, and the University of Dayton, Ohio, in conjunction with the Jet Propulsion Laboratory - California Institute of Technology. I oversee the mission's instrumentation, holding responsibility for designing and selecting the appropriate payload packages for the spacecraft.

Previous Project

Chasing Shadows: Identifying Undiscovered Exoplanets in Kepler’s Historical Data

Undergraduate dissertation, focussing on a reanalysis of Kepler/K2 Light Curves, in a research area with little establishment support. My research was reliant on my own ability to set up such a project from scratch. This involved pitching a unique research topic to the School, networking to source the most relevant supervisor, learning how to process large data sets, selecting a relevant analysis tool, and processing results.
