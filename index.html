About Me

Research Interests

  • Machine Learning Applications for Light Curve Analysis
  • Exoplanet Detection with CubeSat Technology
  • Development of Spacecraft Instrumentation
  • Exoplanetary Atmospherics
  • Transit Curve Anomalies
  • Planet Formation Around Multiple Star Systems

After losing my sense of purpose during service in the Royal Air Force, I turned my attention back to my historic passion: Astronomy! Whilst undertaking my undergraduate research at Birkbeck College, I fell in love with all things exoplanetary, and regained my direction in life. The idea that all of these exoplanets are out in the cosmos, orbiting their stars, and just waiting for us to come along and find them had me totally hooked! To further my goals, I am currently undertaking an MSc in Space Exploration Systems at the University of Leicester. My ultimate goal is to undertake a PhD in exoplanet detection or the development of instrumentation specifically tailored to identifying the smallest, most challenging exoplanets to observe. If you share similar interests or just want to connect — connect with me on LinkedIn!

Away from exoplanets, I am a keen trail runner with the long term goal of running the Western States 100, the Moab 240, and UTMB. Sport is a huge passion, and I am a loyal (and long suffering) supporter of Southampton FC, as well as closely following Formula 1, and Cricket. My partner and I run a small travel channel on YouTube for fun!
