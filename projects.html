DAYBREAK - A Mission to 200 AU

MScConcluded

Role: Instrumentation & Payload Design

Led instrumentation design for the DAYBREAK mission, focusing on heritage instruments adapted for deep-space exploration. I researched and evaluated systems from missions like Voyager, Cassini, MAVEN, and JUNO, applying detailed trade-off analyses to selectt a six-instrument suite comprising of a Magnetometer, Plasma Wave Analyser, Solar Wind Analyser, Cosmic Ray Subsystem, Energetic Neutral Atom Imager, and Pick-Up Ion detector. Whilst selecting these instruments, I ensured they met the mission science goals, while adhering to strict SWaP constraints. My work included modelling magnetic cleanliness, calculating boom requirements, and proposing future refinements. This delivered a cost-effective, science-ready payload for probing the Heliopause and Interstellar Medium.

Chasing Shadows

BScConcluded

An analysis of historical data from the Kepler Space Telescope to identify previously undiscovered exoplanets. I focused on refining searches for short-period planets between 1 – 2 Earth radii, addressing the observed “radius valley” gap in current catalogues. Using the LcTools software suite, I validated my methodology by searching for, and finding, confirmed Kepler Objects of Interest. With the methodology proven, I conducted large-scale analysis on over 10,000 light curves. This work led to the potential identification of a new binary star system. Throughout the project, I optimised data filtering and validation criteria to improve detection reliability, and proposed future observational follow-up to confirm these findings, in an effort to enhance the completeness of Kepler’s legacy dataset.
